Another thing that I have found to work but be risky technically is to
have your OpenSim machine and your grid services on the DMZ of your
router then set your External IP as your Hostname for each.   I think
it would also work for domain name in that scenario.   The key here is
that, for incoming requests that your router doesn't have a matching
outgoing request for, via NAT, it will pipe to the interface set as
the DMZ.   This allows routers that don't support NAT loopback
directly to work.   There are some important security considerations.
  If your box is a Linux box, make sure to only have the services that
you need installed on it and that you keep up with bugtraq and CERT
advisories ( http://seclists.org/ )   If your box is a windows box,
keep up with Windows Update patches.   Even then, there's a chance
that something could get through so be careful.  You might consider
putting a hardware firewall between your router and your machine in
the DMZ to limit your machine's exposure to only the ports that
OpenSimulator uses.
