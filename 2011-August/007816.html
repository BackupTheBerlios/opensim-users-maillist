The misunderstanding here is probably caused by "with OSGrid". If you  
connect your region to OSGrid (i.e. let OSGrid handle accounts, and you  
just run the region services on your own machine) then you indeed can't  
use wifi, as user accounts and inventories will be handled by OSGrid.

If you just want to have your own grid/standalone that can hypergrid to  
OSGrid (and other grids), then you can use Wifi, regardless of whether  
you're running in Grid or Standalone mode.

The 4096 region limit is not related to OSGrid, but is a general OpenSim  
issue. The workaround here is to either have the center of your grid where  
most other grids you want to visit are centered (afaik that's around  
10000,10000), or to have some "bridge" regions you can teleport to first,  
and from there reach grids that are futher away, coordinate-wise, than  
your starting point.

For example: You run your OpenSim regions around 1000,1000, but you want  
to hypergrid to OSGrid, which is centered around 10000,10000. You would  
need an intermediate region that's closer than 4096 to 10000,10000 (your  
destination) and also closer than 4096 to 1000,1000 (your starting point).  
Since 1000+4096=5096 and 10000-4096=5904, we actually need two  
intermediate regions. So you'd have to create a region at around  
4000,4000, and another one around 8000,8000, then teleport to the 4000,  
 from there to 8000, and from there to your destination on OSGrid.
