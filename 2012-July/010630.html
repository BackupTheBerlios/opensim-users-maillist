On Wed, Jul 4, 2012 at 10:08 AM, Fleep Tuque <fleep513 at gmail.com> wrote:

> Hi Tfateme,
>
> Setting up the networking properly is, in my opinion, the most difficult
> and complicated part of running a grid. It's hard to even help someone
> troubleshoot because there are so many variables involved.  The most
> comprehensive documentation available is at:
> http://opensimulator.org/wiki/Network_Settings
>
> Here's a list of questions that might help us figure out what's going on,
> assuming you're running your grid from home:
>
> 1.  Are you running in grid mode (ROBUST) or standalone mode?  Or using
> the Diva Distro?
>
> 2.  Can you paste in the settings for one of your regions from the
> regions.ini file so we can see what you have listed as your internal and
> external IP settings?
>
> 3.  What is the model of your router/modem and is it listed as supporting
> NAT loopback at  http://opensimulator.org/wiki/NAT_Loopback_Routers ?
>
> 4.  Did you set up port forwarding on your router for the appropriate
> ports for your grid configuration, as per the instructions at
> http://opensimulator.org/wiki/Network_Settings#NAT_and_Port_Forwarding ?
>
> 5.  Are you running a firewall on the machine where your grid is hosted?
>  If so, did you open the same ports from question 2 for your firewall?
>
> 6.  Are you running anti-virus software on the machine where your grid is
> hosted?  If so have you turned it off for testing to see if that is the
> problem?
>
> 7.  When you try to connect to your grid from INSIDE your home network,
> what do you see on the robust/opensim console?  Anything at all?
>
> 8.  When you have a friend try to connect to your grid from OUTSIDE your
> home network, what do you see on the robust/opensim console?  Anything at
> all?
>
> 9.  What is the exact error message when the connection fails or exact
> wording when the connection starts to hang on the client when you try to
> connect from both inside and outside your home network?
>
>
> This list isn't exhaustive by any means, but if you can provide us with
> the answers it might give us a place to start helping you troubleshoot.
>
