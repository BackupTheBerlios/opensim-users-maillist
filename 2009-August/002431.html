I have had the same question in the past about installing Hippo.  I found
that if I install it locally, then copy the Hippo installation directory to
a thumb drive (if using Windows) then it runs from the thumb just fine on
other systems where it's not been installed.  Of course, it then creates
some temp directories and such on the system it's running, but no big deal
for me and no need to fool with "download only" installation files on
systems with downloading blocked.

Any thoughts though on what Hippo uses that is "so" special they require
special download considerations that none of the other viewers currently
employ.  Even Meerkat, one of the most advanced by way of special qualities,
such as the Backup ability and the long list of environment settings doesn't
require this segregated download-only approach.

- Len
