On 09/05/11 21:06, Diva Canto wrote:
> Unfortunately, it looks like some bugs affecting the map have crept in 0.7.1, as we moved to make the map work for
> Viewer2. I already fixed them in the branch 0.7.1-dev post-fixes. These bugs might be enough to justify a minor release,
> 0.7.1.1, in a couple of weeks.
>
> ---
>
> In the process of fixing these bugs, one important issue has emerged, pointed to by Teravus. When it comes to the map,
> it looks like the viewers behave strangely for regions whose coordinates are above 4096. Some important things they
> request below 4096 aren't requested above 4096. Yes, it's the 4096 viewer bug again, this time affecting more than just
> teleports -- the map itself.
>
> Specifically, above 4096, when you open the map you only see the regions -8 to +8 from your current region, even when
> there are regions beyond that. (This -/+8 value is a hack provided by OpenSim, because by default you would see only the
> current region and nothing else) This behavior can be seen in OSGrid, for example. If you then click on a tile beyond
> that -/+ 8 square, you get another -/+ 4 tiles around the one you clicked (again, -/+4 hack provided by OpenSim). In
> grids below 4096, the map behaves normal, you see all regions around.
>
> This worries me, as I think a functional map is a very good thing indeed. OSGrid being in 10000's, it pushes a lot of
> other worlds towards that number too, which means that none of those worlds above 4096 will have issue-free maps. And I
> hate to see hacks being introduced in OpenSim just to go around strange viewer limitations. So, I'm bummed with today's
> discovery.
>
